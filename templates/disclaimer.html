Important Medical Disclaimer

Last updated: 1 January 2026

🚨 Medical Emergency?
If you are experiencing a medical emergency — chest pain, difficulty breathing, stroke symptoms, severe injury, or any life-threatening situation — do not use this app. Call 999 (UK) or your local emergency number immediately.

Not a Medical Service

HealthGuardian is a health monitoring and educational tool only. It is not a medical device, clinical diagnostic tool, or healthcare service. HealthGuardian is not registered or approved by the MHRA (UK), FDA (US), CE (EU), or any other medical regulatory body.

Nothing on HealthGuardian — including AI pattern analysis, symptom checker results, AI chat responses, health scores, alerts, or any other feature — constitutes or should be treated as medical advice, medical diagnosis, or medical treatment.

AI Limitations

Our AI features are trained on general health data and are designed to identify broad patterns. They have significant limitations:

  • They cannot account for your complete medical history
  • They cannot physically examine you
  • They may be inaccurate or miss important conditions
  • They are not validated as clinical diagnostic tools
  • The 92% accuracy claim refers to pattern anomaly detection, not disease diagnosis
✅ HealthGuardian IS useful for:
  • Tracking how you feel over time and identifying personal trends
  • Building awareness of your health patterns
  • Having more informed conversations with your doctor
  • General educational health information
  • Motivation to maintain healthy habits
❌ HealthGuardian is NOT a substitute for:
  • A doctor's diagnosis or professional medical opinion
  • Prescribed medication or medical treatment
  • Emergency medical services
  • Mental health professional support
  • Any regulated medical device or service

Always Consult a Doctor

Never delay seeking professional medical advice, never disregard a doctor's advice, and never make medical decisions based solely on HealthGuardian. If you have concerns about a symptom or health pattern, speak to a qualified healthcare professional.

In the UK, you can contact your GP, call NHS 111 for non-emergency medical advice, or go to A&E for urgent (but non-life-threatening) issues.

Mental Health

If you are experiencing mental health difficulties, please reach out to a professional. In the UK: Samaritans: 116 123 (free, 24/7) | MIND: 0300 123 3393 | Crisis Text Line: Text SHOUT to 85258.
